By Public Transport

If you don't drive or cycle, Scotland also has a reliable network of Public Transport that you can use to get out and about. Here, we have handpicked some places where you can travel to on Scotlands train, bus & tram network.



Edinburgh: Scotland's Capital

Travelling to Edinburgh, is only a 45 minute journey by train from Glasgow Queen Street, or 90 minutes by bus from Glasgow Buchanan Bus Station. Being Scotland's capital city, there is plenty to do in Edinburgh, including:

  • Edinburgh Castle
  • Edinburgh Zoo
  • The Scottish Parliment
  • The National Museum of Scotland
  • The Palace of Holyroodhouse
  • Arthur's Seat
  • Scottish National Gallery
  • Royal Botanic Gardens
  • Princes Street
  • and more!

Loch Lomond

45 minutes travel by train from Glasgow Central Station is Loch Lomond, one of the most famous lakes in southern Scotland.
Part of the Loch Lomond and The Trossachs National Park, the surrounding highlands area is home to red deer and oak woodlands. On the eastern shore, footpaths and cycle trails criss-cross craggy Ben Lomond mountain and the smaller Conic Hill. A popular dog walking spot, Loch Lomond is a great destination for hiking, hill walking and family days out. It is also home to the SEA LIFE Loch Lomond Aquarium, which is only a short walk from Balloch train station.
